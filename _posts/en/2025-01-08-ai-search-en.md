Anthropic distinguishes between the concepts of "workflow" and "agent" in the context of AI systems and how they operate:

1. **Workflow:**
   - A **workflow** refers to a structured sequence of tasks or processes designed to achieve a specific goal. In AI, this typically involves a series of predefined steps, where each step depends on defined inputs and produces outputs. Workflows are often linear and rigid in structure, with a clear flow of control from one step to the next.
   - **Example:** A typical workflow might involve an AI system that collects data, performs data cleaning, trains a model, and then deploys the model into production. Each of these steps is done in a defined order to reach the desired outcome.

2. **Agent:**
   - An **agent**, on the other hand, is a more autonomous and flexible entity. In AI, an agent is an entity that perceives its environment, makes decisions based on that perception, and takes actions to achieve its goals. Agents are designed to operate independently, adapting and learning from their interactions with the environment.
   - Unlike workflows, which follow a fixed sequence of steps, agents are dynamic and can adjust their behavior based on varying inputs. They are capable of modifying their actions as they encounter new scenarios.
   - **Example:** An AI agent could be a chatbot that learns and adapts its responses based on user interactions or a recommendation system that adjusts recommendations according to changing user preferences.

### Key Differences:
- **Structure:** A workflow is more rigid and follows a predefined sequence, while an agent is flexible and adapts based on its environment and goals.
- **Autonomy:** Agents typically operate autonomously, learning and adapting over time, while workflows follow fixed steps with little to no real-time decision-making.
- **Purpose:** Workflows are used to automate a sequence of tasks, while agents focus on decision-making and achieving goals through interactions with their environment.

In summary, **workflows** are structured sequences of tasks, while **agents** are autonomous systems that make decisions and adapt to achieve their goals.
