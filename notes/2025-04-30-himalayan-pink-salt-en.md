## 🌄 What is Himalayan Pink Salt?

**Himalayan Pink Salt** is a type of **rock salt (halite)** mined primarily from the **Khewra Salt Mine** in the Punjab region of **Pakistan**, located near the mighty **Himalayas**. It is believed to have formed over **250 million years ago** from ancient sea salt deposits that were crystallized by volcanic activity and protected from modern pollution.

The salt is known for its **distinct pink color**, ranging from soft pink to deep reddish hues, which comes from **trace minerals** like iron oxide (rust), magnesium, potassium, calcium, and others.

---

## ⚛️ Composition

While regular table salt is mostly **sodium chloride (NaCl)** — typically around 97–99% — Himalayan pink salt contains **up to 84 trace minerals**, including:

- Iron (responsible for the pink/red color)
- Magnesium
- Potassium
- Calcium
- Zinc
- Selenium
- Iodine (in small amounts)

These minerals are present in **very small quantities**, so while they contribute to flavor and color, their nutritional impact is minimal unless consumed in large amounts (which is not recommended due to sodium content).

## 🏔️ Origin & Mining

- Formed during the evaporation of ancient seas and later compressed under intense pressure from the forming Himalayan Mountains.
- Mined mainly from the **Khewra Salt Mine**, the second-largest salt mine in the world.
- The mine was discovered by Alexander the Great’s troops in 326 BC when they noticed horses licking the salty rocks.

## 🧂 Uses of Himalayan Pink Salt

### 1. **Culinary Use**
- **Cooking & Seasoning**: Used as a finishing salt or in cooking for its mild flavor and aesthetic appeal.
- **Salt Blocks**: Large slabs used for cooking, grilling, or serving sushi, meats, fruits, and cheeses. They retain cold and heat well.
- **Salt Grinders & Shakers**: Popular in kitchens worldwide for both taste and visual appeal.
- **Brining & Curing**: Used in preserving meats and vegetables.

### 2. **Health & Wellness**
- **Salt Lamps**: Believed by some to emit negative ions that purify air and improve mood, although scientific evidence is limited.
- **Bath Salt**: Dissolved in warm water for relaxation and skin health.
- **Tongue Scrub/Brushing**: Some people use it for oral hygiene.
- **Salt Inhalers**: Used in salt therapy (halotherapy) for respiratory issues.

### 3. **Decorative & Practical Items**
- Carved into lamps, candle holders, cutting boards, mortars, and pestles.
- Used in spas for massage treatments and foot scrubs.

## ⚠️ Considerations & Myths

- **Not a significant source of nutrients**: While rich in trace minerals, you would need to consume large quantities (dangerously high in sodium) to derive any notable benefit.
- **No proven medical miracles**: Claims about air-purifying salt lamps or detoxifying effects are largely anecdotal.
- **Still Sodium**: Like regular salt, excessive consumption can lead to **high blood pressure** and other health problems.
