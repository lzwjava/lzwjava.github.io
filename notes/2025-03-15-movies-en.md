Determining the "10 greatest movies of all time" is a subjective task, as greatness can be judged by various criteria such as critical acclaim, cultural impact, technical innovation, or emotional resonance. However, the following list highlights films that are widely regarded as masterpieces, spanning different genres, eras, and cultures. Each has left a significant mark on cinema and continues to inspire filmmakers and audiences alike.

### 1. **Citizen Kane (1941)**  
   - **Director**: Orson Welles  
   - **Why**: Often cited as the greatest film ever made, *Citizen Kane* revolutionized cinema with its innovative use of deep focus cinematography, non-linear storytelling, and a complex exploration of its titular character. It set a new benchmark for visual and narrative creativity.

### 2. **The Godfather (1972)**  
   - **Director**: Francis Ford Coppola  
   - **Why**: This crime epic is a storytelling masterpiece, blending powerful performances with deep themes of power, family, and morality. Its influence on cinema and popular culture is immense, making it a defining work of the 20th century.

### 3. **Seven Samurai (1954)**  
   - **Director**: Akira Kurosawa  
   - **Why**: A landmark in world cinema, this Japanese samurai epic combines thrilling action with profound humanism. Its innovative techniques, like slow motion, and its influence on countless films make it a timeless classic.

### 4. **The Shawshank Redemption (1994)**  
   - **Director**: Frank Darabont  
   - **Why**: This tale of hope and friendship, adapted from Stephen King’s novella, has grown from a modest release to one of the most beloved films ever. Its emotional depth and universal appeal ensure its enduring popularity.

### 5. **Spirited Away (2001)**  
   - **Director**: Hayao Miyazaki  
   - **Why**: A stunning animated masterpiece, *Spirited Away* showcases breathtaking visuals and a rich, imaginative story about growth and identity. It proves animation’s power as a profound artistic medium.

### 6. **Some Like It Hot (1959)**  
   - **Director**: Billy Wilder  
   - **Why**: This comedy classic delivers timeless humor through sharp wit and memorable performances. Its playful take on gender roles and identity keeps it fresh and relevant decades later.

### 7. **2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)**  
   - **Director**: Stanley Kubrick  
   - **Why**: A groundbreaking sci-fi epic, this film redefined the genre with its pioneering special effects and philosophical depth. Its exploration of human evolution and technology remains captivating and influential.

### 8. **Singin' in the Rain (1952)**  
   - **Directors**: Gene Kelly, Stanley Donen  
   - **Why**: A joyful celebration of cinema, this musical features iconic dance sequences and a charming story about Hollywood’s transition to sound. Its technical brilliance and charm make it unforgettable.

### 9. **Psycho (1960)**  
   - **Director**: Alfred Hitchcock  
   - **Why**: Hitchcock’s psychological thriller reshaped horror with its suspenseful direction, shocking twists, and innovative editing. Its influence on suspense and thriller genres is unmatched.

### 10. **Parasite (2019)**  
   - **Director**: Bong Joon-ho  
   - **Why**: This modern classic blends dark comedy and social commentary on class inequality with masterful storytelling. Its historic Oscar wins and global acclaim mark it as a defining film of the 21st century.

This list reflects a balance of cinematic milestones from different decades, genres (drama, comedy, sci-fi, horror, musical, animation), and countries (United States, Japan, South Korea). While subjective, these films are celebrated for their innovation, emotional impact, and lasting legacy in the world of cinema.
