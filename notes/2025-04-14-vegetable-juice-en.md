Drinking vegetable and fruit juices, including tomato juice, can be beneficial due to their nutritional content, but it's important to consider the sugar content and calorie density. Here's a breakdown:

## Benefits of Vegetable and Fruit Juices

- **Nutritional Value**: Juices like tomato, carrot, and orange are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. For example, tomato juice is high in vitamin C and lycopene, which can enhance immune function and protect against certain cancers[1][7].
- **Health Benefits**: Consuming these juices may improve gut health, boost the immune system, and support heart health[1][2].

## Sugar and Calorie Concerns

- **Sugar Content**: Even 100% fruit and vegetable juices contain natural sugars, which can be high in calories. A serving size of 200 ml typically contains about 16–24 grams of sugar[6].
- **Calorie Density**: A juice with 195 calories per 100 ml is quite calorie-dense. For comparison, a typical fruit juice might have around 40-60 calories per 100 ml[5][6].
- **Health Risks**: Excessive sugar intake, even from natural sources, can lead to health issues like obesity, diabetes, and heart disease if not balanced with other dietary components[5][6].

## NFC Juices

- **Definition**: NFC stands for "Not From Concentrate," meaning the juice is made directly from fresh fruits or vegetables without reconstitution from concentrates[4].
- **Benefits**: NFC juices retain more fiber and nutrients compared to juices made from concentrates, making them a better choice for those seeking to maximize nutritional benefits[4].

## Recommendations

- **Moderation**: It's advisable to consume vegetable and fruit juices in moderation due to their sugar content. The World Health Organization recommends limiting daily free sugar intake to less than 10% of total energy intake[6].
- **Balance**: Ensure that your diet includes a variety of whole foods to balance out the sugar intake from juices.
- **Choose Wisely**: Opt for NFC juices when possible, as they tend to retain more nutrients and fiber compared to other types of juices[4].

In summary, while vegetable and fruit juices can be part of a healthy diet, it's crucial to be mindful of their sugar content and calorie density. Moderation and a balanced diet are key to enjoying these beverages without adverse health effects.
