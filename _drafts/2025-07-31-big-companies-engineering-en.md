Big companies are good at its strict policy control and management. Big companies are good at performing careful checking and thoughrough software release evaluation.

But a lot of engineering can't be settle downed into plain rule. The simplicity design and optimization for every methods or every java classes are not easily to be checked by rules.

SonarQueue Scan is good thing, the high testing coverage is good thing. But a lot of software design or engineering can't be that simply measured. 

The quality of APIs, the easy use of functionalityes can't be easily evaluated. 

The parameters of methods can't be easily evaluated. The design of function, branch strategy, development strattegy are not easily been measured and the naming. 

Alibaba has its java guide, and google , plantier has its java format. It is good. But not all the things in java project can't be automatially checked by code.

About testing, it is ture. There are a lot of auto testing tool. But no all test strategy, design, philosohy, technique will have some fixed rules. 

About product, it is true. There are a lot of A/B testing, data-driven product develpment. But no all product tech, skills, insight can't be measured by fixed rules. 

Why I want to discuss about this? Because it means that our value lies in these sectors. What's things that big companies are not good at? So we can help them do.

Why I mention Engineering in big companies instead of engineering in companies? Actualy, it is not just about big companies or small startups. They are formed with people, just the number of employees has some difference.

Engineering in big companies is less diverse than startups.
