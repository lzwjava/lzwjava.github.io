After Yin Wang claim the myopia reversal method in his [blog](https://www.yinwang.org/blog-cn/2022/02/22/myopia), there are some opposite views on this. I happened to read some. 

And also from 2022, I have conduct that experiement for 3 years, just like this [paper](http://localhost:4000/vision-restoration-en) says. 

I am easy to know what is right or what is not. It is wrong to say heredity is the primary risk factor for myopia. In todd bector's Youtube [Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x5Efg42-Qn0). In the introduction, there is a paragraph. 

> Myopia, or near-sightedness, is generally assumed to be an irreversible, genetically determined condition that can only be ameliorated with corrective lenses or surgery. Its prevalence is 30-40% in the U.S. and Europe, and more than 50% in some Asian countries, but it is rare in Africa and in pre-industrial cultures. The incidence of myopia correlates with IQ, school achievement, and industrialization, suggesting that an environmental factor is at work—namely, near-work.

For my experience, I can say this is right. My father has 3 brothers. Three of 4 siblings doesn't have myopia while one have. And the young brother of my father has myopia while having highest education, probably graduated from bachelor. They was born in 1960s and 1970s.

For myself, I start to have myopia when I am taking junior high school around 2009. At that time, I probably have around 200 degrees. I know that when I sleep night and play mobile phone like Nokia while laying in the bed without lights on. After a summar like that, I feel that my eyesight become poor. 

I happen to know some of the author of opposite views in real life. They also do some blogging. After I have done 3 years' experiement, and Yin Wang try so many ways to reverse myopia. The first thought when I see such opposite view on the Internet is that we don't give a shit. They don't know how much effort we have made, how we rejected our ideas or start again, how we adjust our experiment, how we mulling over all these. 

We are not disrespectful for the opposite view. Science is science. It doesn't matter that there are 2 billion people have myopia. And a large of them use the eyeglasses with full scription that can see 5m away black blackboard to use mobile phones a lot. That's why their myopia become deepen and deepen.

One thing easy to know that whether Yin Wang says is truth that just put down your glasses. If your myopia that is just 175 or 200 degrees, you can see your mobile phone without the need of glasses. And if you are wearing the eye glasses with full descrption, you can put mobile phone far away, like 80 cm or 1 meter, you probably still can see the words in the screen. 

I can see that one of big mistakes is that they don't do hands-on experiment while we do a lot and think about them a lot. They just say the book say how and how.

In the corporate world, I can see this scenario too. In a team, engineers may come out solutions. However, half of them probably doesn't work. We need to try the solutions to see what happens. It is just like tens of thousands of times I think that code should work but testing failed in my 10 years software engineering experince.

From the hands-on aspecdt, we actually can do a lot of filtering. We better hire, communicate, work with those people who are more hands-on. 

Of course, being wrong in myopia is normal. The majority never think about or suspect this. In the company, those who like hands-on will probably be better leaders. Steve jobs once hightlight the importance of highly performant individual contributor. 

So when judging the quality or truthness of opinions, we can check its hands-on degree. Does the author do a lot of experiment?

And I can see that the people who do a lot of hands-on experiment are actually don't hold their opinions too much. I know there still doubt in reversing myopia. What degree is best for reversing myopia, whether the 175 degress less, or 200 degress les? I wrote a paper about this, [Natural Vision Restoration: The Principle of "Just Barely Clear"](https://lzwjava.github.io/barely-clear-en).

It is really easy to talk but harder to perform actual experiment and work out a theory, and then test again, just like writing code.

It is easy to make mistakes without hands-on. Keeping this on heart probably save us tons of trouble as we reduce our mistakes.
